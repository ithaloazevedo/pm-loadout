# PM Flow

This is the default end-to-end product flow. Juninho should enter at the user's current stage, not always at the beginning.

## Flow

1. Clarify the mission
   - Skills: `brainstorming`, `bias-check`
   - Output: framed problem or opportunity

2. Understand users and jobs
   - Skills: `interview`, `user-interview`, `jtbd-map`, `user-needs-map`
   - Output: needs, jobs, pains, motivations, evidence

3. Map opportunities
   - Skills: `ost-builder`, `assumption-test`
   - Output: opportunity tree, riskiest assumptions, learning plan

4. Choose bets
   - Skills: `ice-score`, `gist-plan`, `wardley-map`, `cynefin-classify`, `devils-advocate`
   - Output: prioritized bet, rationale, trade-offs

5. Shape work
   - Skills: `clickup-spec` (ClickUp) or `linear-spec` (Linear), `linear-issues`
   - Output: roadmap item / initiative, discovery project, delivery project, subtask / PM issue, acceptance shape

6. Check quality and readiness
   - Skills: `service-check`, `usability-check`, `launch-tier`
   - Output: quality risks, launch tier, mitigations

7. Measure and learn
   - Skills: `metrics-detect`, `retrospective`
   - Output: success signals, learning loop, next iteration

## Flow Principles

- Discovery and delivery can overlap, but evidence should not be invented to make delivery feel ready.
- A good PM flow leaves a decision trail: why this problem, why this solution, why now.
- Every artifact should name its evidence confidence.
- Every recommendation should include what would change the recommendation.
